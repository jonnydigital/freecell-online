# Content Strategy

*March 7, 2026*

## Goal

Build a repeatable content engine that grows:

- organic traffic
- internal link depth
- topical authority around solitaire
- time on site and ad impressions

This is not a "write random blog posts" strategy.

It is a controlled publishing system with four content buckets:

1. **Money pages**: game pages and high-intent support pages that directly lead to play
2. **Support pages**: rules, strategy, comparison, FAQ, and explainer pages
3. **Authority pages**: broader solitaire articles that widen the keyword surface
4. **Linkbait / data pages**: surveys, rankings, and unusual angles that earn links

## Core Strategy

### 1. Publish by Cluster, Not by Random Topic

Every important game needs a cluster:

- playable page
- rules / how-to-play
- strategy
- FAQ or tips
- comparison pages
- one or two distinctive deep-dive pages

Clusters beat isolated posts because they:

- strengthen internal linking
- build topical authority
- make spoke domains viable later
- reduce cannibalization

### 2. Prioritize Search Intent in This Order

1. **Play intent**: "play freecell", "spider solitaire online", "klondike solitaire online"
2. **Learn intent**: "how to play", "strategy", "rules", "tips", "is it solvable"
3. **Compare intent**: "freecell vs spider", "draw 1 vs draw 3", "1 suit vs 4 suit"
4. **Browse intent**: "best solitaire games", "types of solitaire", "easiest solitaire games"
5. **Linkbait intent**: surveys, rankings, workplace / relaxation / cognitive angle content

### 3. Keep the Portfolio Roles Clean

## Publishing Mix

Target mix for the next 60 days:

- **50%** money/support pages
- **30%** authority pages
- **20%** linkbait / data pages

If the team starts publishing mostly broad blog content, the strategy is drifting.

## Daily Cadence

### Minimum Daily Output

Each day should produce one of these:

- 1 publishable money/support page
- or 2 publishable authority pages
- or 1 refresh + 1 new article brief + 1 finished draft

### Weekly Output Target

- 3 high-intent money/support pages
- 2 authority blog posts
- 1 comparison page
- 1 refresh / update pass on an older page
- 1 daily pipeline run every weekday

## Editorial Guardrails

- No AI slop
- No em dashes
- Grade 8 reading level
- No filler intros
- Every page must earn its existence
- Every page must link to a relevant game page
- Every page should include a Key Takeaways section
- Most evergreen pages should include FAQ-ready questions
- Use first-hand clarity and examples, not empty "benefits" copy

## Content Types and When to Use Them

### Money page

Use for:

- rules pages
- strategy pages
- game-specific explainers
- comparison pages close to game intent

Definition:

- tied closely to a game query
- easy to internally link to gameplay
- should plausibly convert a searcher into a player

### Authority page

Use for:

- browse-intent listicles
- taxonomy content
- broader solitaire explainers

Definition:

- broader than one game
- useful for the hub
- often a bridge into multiple game pages

### Linkbait / data page

Use for:

- rankings
- state-by-state studies
- original survey content
- unusual cultural or workplace angles

Definition:

- built to attract links, press, or shares
- may not convert directly
- should still route users to game pages

## Internal Linking Rules

Every article should link to:

- the most relevant playable game page
- 2-4 closely related support pages
- 1 broader hub or taxonomy page when relevant

Examples:

- FreeCell strategy content -> `/`, `/solver`, `/deals`, `/daily-freecell`
- Spider support content -> `/spider`, `/freecell-vs-spider`, future `/spider/strategy`
- Klondike support content -> future `/klondike`, `/freecell-vs-klondike`
- Hub listicles -> all relevant game pages

## Daily Decision Framework

When choosing what to publish today, score candidate ideas on:

- demand
- fit with owned domains
- fit with an existing or near-term game
- internal linking value
- effort
- risk of thin or duplicate content

Prefer:

- ideas that strengthen an existing cluster
- ideas that help a future spoke launch cleanly
- ideas with obvious internal links and FAQs

Avoid:

- random viral topics with no solitaire connection
- duplicate comparison pages with weak differentiation
- broad posts that never route to gameplay

## What "Mass Content" Should Mean Here

It should mean:

- many good pages produced from a controlled system
- fast drafting with strict editing
- daily opportunity review
- clear clusters and link structure

It should not mean:

- publishing thin AI text
- flooding the site with near-duplicate pages
- launching spoke domains before the content clusters exist
