## Appendix: Raw Data Reference

### FreeCell Solvability Stats
- Original 32,000 MS deals: 31,999 solvable (only #11982 impossible)
- Extended 1 million deals: 8 unsolvable
- Full 8.6 billion Freecell Pro range: 102,075 impossible (~1 in 84,000)
- 6 deals impossible even with 5 freecells
- Baker's Game: 74.3% solvable (10 million deals tested)
- ForeCell (FreeCell predecessor): 85.6% solvable (10 million deals)

### Spider Solvability Stats
- 4-suit: ~33% win rate with good play (Hoyle's)
- Estimated 1 in 3,000 random deals may be truly unwinnable
- Known unwinnable: #14934, possibly #1748
- #10957 was thought unwinnable but solved in 2020

### Klondike Win Rate Stats
- Draw-1, unlimited passes: ~52% (AI)
- Draw-3, unlimited passes: ~18% (AI)
- Skilled human player: ~43%
- Draw-1, single pass: ~3.3% (Hoyle's: 1 in 30)
- Thoughtful Klondike (all visible, draw-3): 81.9%

### Key Historical Dates
- 1783: Earliest known solitaire rules (German game anthology)
- ~1870: First English solitaire book (Lady Adelaide Cadogan)
- 1920s: Baker family learns a card game from an Englishman
- 1945: Swedish book describes FreeCell relative "Napoleon in St. Helena"
- 1949: Spider solitaire originated
- 1968: Martin Gardner describes Baker's Game in Scientific American
- 1978: Paul Alfille creates FreeCell on PLATO
- 1988: Jim Horne creates DOS shareware FreeCell ($10); also joins Microsoft
- 1990: Microsoft Solitaire (Klondike) ships with Windows 3.0
- 1991: First Windows FreeCell in Entertainment Pack 2
- 1992: FreeCell used to test Win32s compatibility
- 1994-1995: Internet FreeCell Project (Dave Ring, 100+ volunteers)
- 1995: FreeCell bundled with Windows 95
- 1998: Spider added to Windows via Plus! 98
- 2001: Windows XP extends FreeCell to 1 million deals
- 2006: NYC worker fired for playing Solitaire (Bloomberg incident)
- 2016: Theodore Pringle analyzes all 8.6 billion Freecell Pro deals
- 2019: Microsoft Solitaire inducted into World Video Game Hall of Fame
- 2020: 35 million monthly active Microsoft Solitaire players
