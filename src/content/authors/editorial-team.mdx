The Solitaire Stack Editorial Team is the masthead behind the network of solitaire sites we publish, including PlayFreeCellOnline.com, SolitaireStack.com, and the spoke domains still rolling out. We are the editors who assign, review, and maintain every page you read across the network. We are not a byline you will see on a hands-on strategy piece or a data study - you will see our desks for that. Our job is to set the standard and hold every page to it.

### Why the network exists

Roughly one hundred million people play solitaire every month. The web that greets them is thin. A typical search for "how to play FreeCell" returns three kinds of pages: copy-paste rule sheets that were written in 2008 and never updated, SEO blog posts that hedge on every claim, and game pages that bury an ad for a mobile app under a paragraph of filler. We started this network because that gap felt unacceptable. A game played by that many people deserves real reference material, real strategy content, and real research - the kind of thing a curious player can read for an hour and come away genuinely better at the game.

### The four desks

The writing is done by four specialty desks, each with its own editor and its own voice.

- **The Strategy Desk** covers tactics, probability, and decision-making. They work from play and from simulation data.
- **The History Desk** covers origins, variants, and the cultural story of how these games traveled from nineteenth-century European salons to Windows 3.0.
- **The Rules Desk** covers canonical rules, teaching, and how-to writing. They test their explanations on real beginners.
- **The Research Desk** runs the simulations, builds the solvers, and publishes the data that the Strategy Desk leans on.

Our role is to coordinate across desks, commission cross-cutting pieces (a variant page usually needs all four), and keep the house style and fact-checking process consistent.

### Editorial process

Every article goes through a fixed pipeline:

1. **Research.** The assigned desk gathers sources, plays the game, or runs the simulation.
2. **Draft.** The writer drafts at length without worrying about word count.
3. **Desk review.** Another editor on the same desk reads for accuracy and voice.
4. **Fact-check.** Claims with numbers, dates, or attributions get verified against primary sources.
5. **Copy edit.** House style, clarity, headline.
6. **Publish.**
7. **Revisit.** Every evergreen page is reviewed on a rolling schedule. Dates on articles are real update dates, not auto-bumped timestamps.

We correct errors in public. If you find one, write to us at editors@solitairestack.com and we will fix it and credit the reader who flagged it when they want credit.

### What we will not do

We will not run content that was generated without a human editor in the loop. We will not publish strategy claims we have not tested. We will not repeat folk etymology as fact, and we will not quote win-rate statistics without a methodology note. We will not accept paid placements or sponsored content that is not clearly labeled. We will not write a "top 10" listicle unless we have actually ranked ten things for a reason the reader cares about.

### What we care about

We care about the reader who opens the site at 11pm looking for one specific answer, the reader who wants to go deep on a variant they have never tried, the reader who grew up on Windows Solitaire and wants to understand why the Microsoft version felt different, and the reader who wants to know whether a particular deal is genuinely unwinnable or they just missed something. We write for all of them.

### Get in touch

Tips, corrections, primary sources, disagreements, and reader questions are all welcome at editors@solitairestack.com. If your question is specific to a desk, you can reach each team directly through the desk pages.
