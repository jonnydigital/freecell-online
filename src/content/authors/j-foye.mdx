J. Foye is the editor and founder of the Solitaire Stack network. The role is part publisher, part product lead, and part final editor: setting the standards for the sites, shaping the tools players use, and making sure the reference material is maintained as the portfolio grows.

### What I oversee

The day-to-day work is editorial direction across SolitaireStack.com, PlayFreeCellOnline.com, and the related solitaire properties. That includes deciding which reference pages are worth publishing, setting the house style, reviewing major updates before they go live, and keeping the public editorial standards, fact-checking policy, correction policy, and methodology pages aligned with the actual work.

### Why this network exists

Solitaire is one of the most-played game families in the world, but much of the open web still treats it as disposable filler. The goal of this network is different: build fast playable games, pair them with useful rules and strategy pages, and publish data or methodology whenever a claim needs support.

The editorial standard is simple. A page should help a player solve a real problem, understand a rule, make a better decision, or verify a claim. If it does not do one of those things, it should not be published.

### Editorial responsibilities

I sign off on major reference pages, own the publishing calendar, and make final calls on corrections or source disputes. The specialist desks handle the deepest work in rules, strategy, history, and research, but the founder/editor role is accountable for making those pages consistent, accurate, and useful as a body of work.
