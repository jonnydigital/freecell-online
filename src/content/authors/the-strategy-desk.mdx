The Strategy Desk covers the decision-making layer of every game in the Solitaire Stack network. When a reader wants to know which card to move, when to commit a foundation play, or why their FreeCell deal keeps stalling on the fourth column, that is our beat.

### What we do

We analyze solitaire the way chess writers analyze middlegames: position by position, tradeoff by tradeoff, with the math shown where it matters. Most published solitaire strategy falls into one of two failure modes. The first is advice so vague it cannot be acted on - "plan ahead," "think before you move," "try to build long sequences." The second is advice so deal-specific it does not generalize. We try to live in the middle: concrete principles that hold across deals, with the exceptions called out.

### How we work

Strategy claims on the network come from two sources: deep play and simulation data. We log thousands of hands, we review our own losses, and we collaborate closely with the Research Desk. When we recommend a FreeCell opening move, the Research Desk has usually run a win-rate comparison across matched deal pools. When we say "free the Ace of Spades before committing the red seven," we can show you why the reverse order costs you win rate in the long run.

We write strategy in three flavors:

- **Principles.** Short, durable rules of thumb that apply across deals. ("In Spider 4-suit, an empty column is worth more than any single card move.")
- **Decision trees.** When a situation has two or three common branches, we walk through each one.
- **Case studies.** A full deal, move by move, with the reasoning attached. These are the hardest pieces to write and the most rewarding.

### What we own on the network

- **FreeCell.** Opening theory, supermove math, free-cell management, the mid-game transition, and endgame technique.
- **Klondike.** Draw-1 versus draw-3 strategy, stock-cycling logic, when to redeal, the foundation-commit question.
- **Spider.** Suit-count strategy (1-suit, 2-suit, 4-suit are materially different games), empty-column valuation, stock timing.
- **Baker's Game, Eight Off, and the FreeCell variants.** Rule differences drive different strategy. We cover each one.
- **Endgame across all variants.** Reading the board late in a hand is a skill that transfers between games.

### What we will not do

We will not hand out rules of the form "always X" or "never Y" unless we have the data. We will not recycle strategy advice from other sites without testing it ourselves. We will not tell you what move to make without explaining the alternative you rejected.
