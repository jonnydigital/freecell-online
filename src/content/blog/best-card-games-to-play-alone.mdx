Card games are not just for groups. Some of the best card games ever designed are meant to be played alone. The solitaire family includes dozens of solo card games, each with a unique mix of strategy, luck, and difficulty.

Here are seven of the best single-player card games, ranked by overall quality and fun.

## 1. FreeCell - The Thinking Player's Solitaire

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Win rate:** ~82% | **Time per game:** 5-10 minutes

FreeCell stands apart from every other solitaire game because all 52 cards are dealt face-up. There is no hidden information and almost no luck. Every deal is a pure logic puzzle, and nearly every deal (about 99.999%) is solvable.

Four free cells give you temporary storage to maneuver cards around the board. The challenge is figuring out the right sequence of moves to build all four suits from Ace to King.

**Best for:** Players who want a genuine strategic challenge where skill determines the outcome.

## 2. Klondike - The Original Classic

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Win rate:** ~43% | **Time per game:** 5-15 minutes

Klondike is the solitaire game. When someone says "solitaire" without specifying which kind, they mean Klondike. Seven tableau columns, one stock pile, four foundations. Build alternating-color sequences on the tableau and move completed suits to the foundations.

The mix of face-down cards and stock pile management creates a game where both skill and luck matter. You will win roughly half your games with good play, which hits the sweet spot of challenging but achievable.

**Best for:** Everyone. If you have never played solitaire, start here.

## 3. Spider Solitaire - The Scalable Challenge

**Difficulty:** Easy to Very Hard | **Win rate:** 35-99% | **Time per game:** 10-30 minutes

Spider uses two decks and 10 tableau columns. The goal is to build complete King-to-Ace sequences of the same suit. The game comes in three modes that dramatically change the difficulty:

| Mode | Suits | Win Rate | Difficulty |
|------|-------|----------|------------|
| 1-suit | All one suit | ~99% | Easy |
| 2-suit | Two suits | ~85% | Medium |
| 4-suit | All four suits | ~35% | Very Hard |

This scalability is Spider's greatest strength. You can play a relaxing 1-suit game or tackle the brutally difficult 4-suit mode. Few card games offer this range.

**Best for:** Players who want to adjust difficulty to match their skill level.

## 4. Pyramid Solitaire - The Math Puzzle

**Difficulty:** Medium | **Win rate:** ~40-50% | **Time per game:** 5-10 minutes

Pyramid deals 28 cards in a pyramid shape. Remove pairs of exposed cards that add up to 13 (King = 13, Queen = 12, Jack = 11, etc.). Kings can be removed alone since they already equal 13.

The constant mental addition makes Pyramid feel different from other solitaire games. It exercises a different part of your brain and provides excellent variety in a solitaire rotation.

**Best for:** Players who enjoy number puzzles and want something distinct from standard card stacking games.

## 5. TriPeaks - The Fast-Paced Pick-Up

**Difficulty:** Easy | **Win rate:** ~90% | **Time per game:** 3-5 minutes

TriPeaks lays out cards in three overlapping peaks. Remove cards that are one rank higher or lower than the current waste card. The simple rule makes it instantly accessible, and games fly by in minutes.

The high win rate and quick pace make TriPeaks the perfect coffee-break game. There is genuine strategy in choosing which path through the peaks to take, but the learning curve is gentle.

**Best for:** Quick sessions, beginners, or when you want a guaranteed good time without deep concentration.

## 6. Golf Solitaire - The Minimalist's Choice

**Difficulty:** Easy-Medium | **Win rate:** ~20-30% | **Time per game:** 2-3 minutes

Golf deals seven columns of five cards each. Remove cards from the columns that are one rank above or below the waste card. No suit matching required. The simplicity is the point.

Games are lightning fast and the rules take 30 seconds to learn. The low win rate is offset by how quickly you can start another game. Golf is the solitaire equivalent of a quick sketch versus an oil painting.

**Best for:** Ultra-short sessions and players who prefer simplicity over complexity.

## 7. Canfield - The Gambling Ancestor

**Difficulty:** Hard | **Win rate:** ~15-20% | **Time per game:** 5-10 minutes

Canfield was originally a casino game where you paid $52 to play and received $5 for each card moved to the foundation. The house won on average because the win rate is low, but the game itself is excellent.

A 13-card reserve pile, a rotating stock, and foundations that start on whatever rank happens to be dealt first create a unique challenge. Canfield demands creative problem-solving and rewards experience.

**Best for:** Experienced solitaire players looking for a tough challenge with a fascinating history.

## How to Choose Your Solo Card Game

The right game depends on what you are looking for:

| I want... | Play this |
|-----------|-----------|
| A pure strategy challenge | FreeCell |
| The classic experience | Klondike |
| Adjustable difficulty | Spider |
| A mental math workout | Pyramid |
| Quick, easy fun | TriPeaks |
| Ultra-short games | Golf |
| A hard, unique challenge | Canfield |

Most dedicated solitaire players rotate between 2-3 games. A good starting combination is Klondike for everyday play, FreeCell when you want a real challenge, and TriPeaks when you want to relax.

## Tips for New Solo Card Game Players

1. **Start with the classics.** Klondike and FreeCell have stood the test of time for good reason. Learn these first.
2. **Use the undo button.** Every mistake is a learning opportunity. Undo, analyze what went wrong, and try a different approach.
3. **Play without timers at first.** Speed comes naturally with experience. Focus on making good decisions, not fast ones.
4. **Try difficulty variants.** Most games offer easier and harder modes. Spider's suit modes and Klondike's draw-1 vs draw-3 are great examples.
5. **Track your win rate.** Watching your win rate climb over time is one of the most satisfying aspects of solitaire.
