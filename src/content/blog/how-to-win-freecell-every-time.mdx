Here's the dirty secret of FreeCell: **99.999% of deals are solvable**. If you're losing more than 1 in 5 games, it's not bad luck — it's fixable mistakes. Let's fix them.

## The Win Rate Reality Check

Most casual players win about 40-60% of their games. Expert players push 85-95%. The theoretical maximum is essentially 100% (only [one known deal](/blog/is-every-freecell-game-winnable) out of 32,000 is provably unsolvable).

The gap between 50% and 90% isn't talent — it's method.

## Step 1: Never Move Without a Reason

The biggest mistake losing players make is moving cards because they *can*, not because they *should*. Every move should serve one of these goals:

- **Uncover a buried ace or deuce** (top priority)
- **Create an empty column** (your most powerful resource)
- **Build a sequence** you'll need later
- **Free a cell** that's currently occupied

If a move doesn't advance any of these, don't make it. Patience wins more games than cleverness.

## Step 2: Treat Empty Columns Like Gold

An empty column is worth more than a free cell. Here's why:

- A free cell holds **1 card**
- An empty column holds **an entire sequence**
- Empty columns multiply your [supermove](/glossary) capacity exponentially

The formula: you can move **(1 + free cells) × 2^(empty columns)** cards at once. With 2 free cells and 1 empty column, that's 6 cards. With 2 free cells and 2 empty columns? 12 cards.

**Never fill an empty column without a plan to empty it again.**

## Step 3: Build in the Right Direction

When building tableau sequences, prefer moves that:

1. **Expose cards you need** (aces, deuces, cards matching a foundation sequence)
2. **Consolidate into fewer columns** (freeing up space)
3. **Keep your options open** (don't commit to one approach too early)

A common trap: building a beautiful long sequence on a column that's blocking an ace at the bottom. Pretty, but losing.

## Step 4: Use the Undo Button Strategically

[Undo](/tips) isn't cheating — it's how you explore the decision tree. When you hit a dead end:

1. Undo to the last decision point
2. Try the alternative
3. Compare which path opened more options

Think of it as "branching" — you're testing which timeline leads to a win. Expert players undo dozens of times per game.

## Step 5: Know When to Restart

Not every deal is worth grinding for 20 minutes. If you've explored multiple branches and keep hitting the same wall, restart with a fresh deal. Your time is better spent on a winnable game than fighting an uphill battle.

That said, try at least 3 different approaches before giving up. Many "impossible" positions have a creative solution hiding in an unexpected move order.

## Step 6: Practice the Endgame

The endgame (when auto-complete kicks in) is where games are won or lost. Key endgame principles:

- **Keep foundations balanced** — don't rush one suit to King while others are at 3
- **Free your cells before the final push** — trapped cards in cells are the #1 cause of late-game losses
- **Count your outs** — if you need the 7♠ and it's under 3 cards with 2 free cells, you can get there. If it's under 6 cards with 0 free cells, you can't

## The 90% Formula

Combine everything above into a checklist:

1. ✅ Scan the board for 30 seconds before moving
2. ✅ Every move has a stated purpose
3. ✅ Empty columns stay empty (or get emptied quickly)
4. ✅ Use undo to explore branches
5. ✅ Restart gracefully when stuck after 3 attempts
6. ✅ Balanced foundation building in the endgame

Players who follow this system consistently hit 85-95% win rates. The remaining 5-15% is a mix of genuinely hard deals and the occasional mental slip.
