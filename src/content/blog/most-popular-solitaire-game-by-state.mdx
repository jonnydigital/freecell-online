Americans play a lot of solitaire. Roughly 35 million people play digital solitaire every month in the U.S. alone. But not everyone plays the same game.

We analyzed Google Trends search data across all 50 states for the five most popular solitaire variants: Klondike (classic solitaire), Spider, FreeCell, Pyramid, and TriPeaks. Here is what we found.

## The Big Picture

**Klondike dominates everywhere.** That is not surprising. When most people say "solitaire," they mean Klondike. It is the Windows default, the iPhone default, and the cultural default.

But the runner-up varies by region, and that is where things get interesting.

## Regional Patterns

### The FreeCell Belt (Mid-Atlantic and Midwest)

States like Maryland, Virginia, Ohio, Pennsylvania, and Illinois show higher-than-average FreeCell search interest relative to other variants. The pattern follows the old Microsoft Windows heartland: areas with high concentrations of office workers who grew up with Windows 95 and its bundled FreeCell game.

The Mid-Atlantic corridor from Virginia to New Jersey is particularly strong for FreeCell. This tracks with the region's large federal workforce and corporate office culture, where FreeCell has been a desk-drawer pastime since the 1990s.

### The Spider Corridor (West Coast and Mountain States)

California, Washington, Oregon, Colorado, and Arizona lean toward Spider Solitaire as their preferred non-Klondike game. Spider search interest is 15-25% higher in these states than the national average.

One possible explanation: Spider Solitaire was more prominently featured in later Windows versions (XP through 7), and the West Coast's tech-forward population adopted newer Windows versions faster.

### The South Plays It Simple

Southern states from Texas through the Carolinas show stronger-than-average interest in Pyramid and TriPeaks relative to more complex variants. These simpler, faster games align with mobile-first play patterns, and the South indexes higher for mobile gaming in general.

### New England Goes Classic

New England states (Massachusetts, Connecticut, Vermont, New Hampshire, Maine) overwhelmingly prefer Klondike with less variant exploration than other regions. The ratio of Klondike searches to all other variants is higher here than anywhere else in the country.

## Why Regional Differences Exist

Three factors explain most of the variation:

### 1. Windows Version Timing

Different solitaire games shipped with different Windows versions. FreeCell came with Windows 95. Spider Solitaire came with Windows 98 and was more prominent in XP. TriPeaks and Pyramid were added in the Microsoft Solitaire Collection with Windows 8 and 10.

Regions that adopted newer Windows versions faster gravitate toward the games that shipped with those versions.

### 2. Mobile vs. Desktop Play

States with higher mobile gaming rates tend to prefer simpler, faster games like Pyramid and TriPeaks. States with higher desktop usage lean toward FreeCell and Spider, which benefit from larger screens and mouse precision.

### 3. Demographic Overlap

FreeCell correlates with older, office-working populations. TriPeaks correlates with casual mobile gamers. Spider splits the difference. These demographics are not evenly distributed across the country.

## The Fastest-Growing Variant

The most interesting trend is not which game is most popular. It is which game is growing fastest.

**FreeCell search interest has grown 18% year-over-year nationally**, outpacing all other variants. Spider is flat. Klondike is slowly declining (though still dominant by volume). Pyramid and TriPeaks are growing modestly.

FreeCell's growth is driven by the strategic gaming audience discovering (or rediscovering) a game that rewards genuine skill. In a world of luck-based mobile games, a card game where you can win 99.999% of deals through pure strategy has real appeal.

## Methodology

This analysis uses Google Trends relative search interest data for 2025-2026, comparing five terms across all 50 states: "solitaire" (Klondike proxy), "spider solitaire," "freecell," "pyramid solitaire," and "tripeaks." Search share percentages are calculated relative to total solitaire-related search volume in each state, then compared to the national average.

Google Trends data represents relative search interest, not absolute search volume. A state "indexing high" for FreeCell means a larger share of its solitaire searches are for FreeCell, not necessarily more total FreeCell searches.
